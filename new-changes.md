# My recommended final flow

I would keep your page to **5 major sections**, plus the footer.

### 1. Hero

### 2. "What are you looking for?" pathway

### 3. Property discovery

### 4. "What it's like working with me"

### 5. Personal CTA / conversation

### 6. Footer

You are already very close to this.

The biggest thing I would change is **the wording and order within those sections**.

---

# 1. HERO SECTION

### Your current copy

> Hi, I'm
> Abdullahi
> Idris Gana
> mostly known as AI GANA.
>
> Whether you're renting your first apartment, upgrading to a bigger home, or looking for your next investment, I'll help you find the right property without pressure, confusion, or endless back and forth.
>
> What are you looking for?

### My verdict

**The direction is good.**

The strongest part is:

> "without pressure, confusion, or endless back and forth."

That is psychologically useful because you are not selling property first. You are selling **relief from the unpleasant experience of property searching**.

I would keep that concept.

But I would make one important change.

Your opening currently spends a lot of valuable space telling me **who you are** before telling me **why I should care**.

Your visitor is thinking:

> "I'm looking for a house. Can this person help me?"

So I would restructure it.

### I would use:

> **Hi, I'm Abdullahi Idris Gana.**
> **Mostly known as AI GANA.**
>
> **Finding the right property shouldn't feel this complicated.**
>
> Whether you're renting your first apartment, upgrading to a bigger home, or exploring your next investment, I'll help you make sense of your options and find a property that actually fits your needs.
>
> **[ What are you looking for? ]**
>
> *Usually replies within an hour.*

The important psychological shift is:

**Current:**
"I am a real estate agent who can help you."

**New:**
"You have a problem. I understand it. I can help."

That's more client-centered.

### One thing I would NOT do

Don't put:

> "Find your dream home"

I think your current positioning is better than that generic real estate language.

Your strongest brand territory is **clarity + honesty + human guidance**.

That is more ownable.

---

# 2. YOUR "WHAT ARE YOU LOOKING FOR?" CTA

This is one of the best things on your page.

You have:

> What are you looking for?

Then the user can presumably start a WhatsApp conversation.

I would **not remove this**.

But I would make the CTA itself more psychologically specific.

Instead of:

> **What are you looking for?**

Try:

> **Tell me what you're looking for**

Or:

> **Tell me what you need**

My preference:

### **Tell me what you're looking for**

Because the user is not being asked to "contact an agent."

They're being invited to **describe a problem**.

That's a much lower psychological barrier.

Under it:

> *Not sure what you need yet? That's okay. We'll figure it out together.*

This is important for your brand because you explicitly want to attract people who are still exploring.

That sentence gives permission to engage **before they are ready to buy**.

---

# 3. THE QUOTE

You currently have:

> "My goal isn't to sell you the most expensive property. It's to help you make a decision you'll be happy with."

This is **very good positioning**.

I would keep the idea.

But I would slightly change the wording.

### My version:

> **"My goal isn't to sell you the most expensive property. It's to help you make a decision you'll still feel good about later."**

Why?

"Happy with" is positive.

"Still feel good about later" introduces the concept of **future regret avoidance**.

Real estate is a high-risk, high-anxiety purchase. People aren't only asking:

> "Can I afford this?"

They're also asking:

> "What if I make the wrong decision?"

Your sentence should quietly tell them:

> "I'm here to help you avoid that."

That is powerful.

# 4. YOUR PROPERTY SECTION

You currently have:

> A Few Places You Might Like

Then:

> Swipe or tap cards to explore, then tap WhatsApp to inquire.

Then:

> Sale Listings / Rental Listings

Then an empty state:

> No properties listed in this category right now.

### This is the first place I would make a meaningful change.

"A Few Places You Might Like" sounds like an algorithm or generic property portal.

But your brand is supposed to feel **personal**.

You are AI GANA.

Not PropertyPro.

Not Zillow.

Not a faceless property database.

I would change it to:

### **A few properties worth seeing**

or:

### **Some properties I think you'll like**

My personal choice:

> ## **A few properties worth seeing**
>
> *Browse a few properties I've selected. If something catches your eye, I'll help you take the next step.*

That feels much more human.

You are subtly positioning yourself as a **curator**, not just someone reposting listings.

---

## Important: Your empty state

This is actually a bigger opportunity than it looks.

You currently have:

> No properties listed in this category right now.

That's technically correct.

But psychologically, it creates a dead end.

The visitor has arrived at your property section and been told:

> "Nothing here."

Instead, turn the empty state into a **lead-generation opportunity**.

### Replace it with:

> **Nothing here that fits right now.**
>
> I'm always updating my listings, and the right property may not be published yet.
>
> **Tell me what you're looking for and I'll help you find it.**
>
> **[ Ask AI GANA directly on WhatsApp ]**

Now your lack of listings becomes a reason to contact you.

That is a much better psychological move.

You're essentially saying:

> "Don't wait for me to post your property. Tell me what you need."

That is particularly important for Abuja real estate.

# 5. YOUR "WHAT IT'S LIKE WORKING WITH ME" SECTION

This is, in my opinion, the **most important section on your page**.

Because your website isn't really selling properties.

**It is selling you.**

The properties are the inventory.

**You are the product.**

And I think you've understood that better than many real estate agents.

Your current:

> What It's Like Working With Me

is good.

Keep it.

Your subtitle:

> Clear guidance. Honest advice. Better property decisions.

I like it.

But I would make one small adjustment:

### Current

> Clear guidance. Honest advice. Better property decisions.

### New

> **Clear guidance. Honest advice. No unnecessary pressure.**

Then your philosophy:

> "Before I recommend a property, I try to understand the person."

Excellent.

I would make it even stronger:

> **"Before I recommend a property, I want to understand the person looking for it."**

Then:

> Buying or renting property in Abuja shouldn't feel exhausting.

I would change this to:

> **Buying or renting a property in Abuja shouldn't feel like a guessing game.**

That is a more specific pain.

Then your comparison table.

This is where I would make the **biggest copy improvement**.

Your current table is conceptually good, but the wording should sound less like a marketing framework and more like **your actual behavior**.

I would use:

### **You can expect me to...**

| Instead of...                                | You'll get...                                                     |
| -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pushing you toward the most expensive option | Recommendations based on what actually fits you                   |
| Sending endless listings                     | A shortlist that makes sense for your needs                       |
| Giving you vague advice                      | Clear explanations, even when the answer isn't what you hoped for |
| Making you feel rushed                       | Time to think through your decision                               |
| Disappearing after you enquire               | Someone who stays involved when you need help                     |

That is much stronger.

Why?

Because you're taking abstract values:

> Honest
> Clear
> No pressure

and turning them into **observable behaviors**.

The brain trusts behavior more than adjectives.

Anyone can say:

> "I'm honest."

Very few people say:

> "I won't push you toward the most expensive option."

That's a concrete promise.

# 6. YOUR BIGGEST MISSING ELEMENT: PROOF

This is the one major thing I think you're missing.

Your page tells me:

> "I'm honest."

> "I don't pressure people."

> "I care about finding the right property."

But I haven't yet seen **evidence**.

You need a small amount of proof.

Not a massive testimonials section.

Not five more sections.

Just **one compact proof layer**.

If you have genuine testimonials, add this **inside or immediately after "What It's Like Working With Me."**

### Example:

> ## **Don't just take my word for it**
>
> *"AI GANA took the time to understand what I actually needed instead of just sending me random properties."*
>
> **Name**
> Client

If you don't have testimonials yet, **do not invent them**.

Instead, use evidence from your actual work.

For example:

> ## **How I work**
>
> **I ask questions first.**
> Before recommending a property, I want to understand your budget, location, priorities and what you're actually looking for.
>
> **I narrow the options.**
> You shouldn't have to dig through hundreds of listings to find three that make sense.
>
> **I help you think it through.**
> If something doesn't look right, I'll tell you. The goal isn't just to find a property. It's to make a decision you understand.

This is essentially **proof through process**.

And I think this is the best option for you **right now**, until you have enough real testimonials.

# My final diagnosis

I **would not make your page longer**.

I would actually make it **slightly shorter**.

The screenshot already has the bones of a very interesting personal real estate brand.

Your biggest opportunity isn't:

> "Add more sections."

It's:

> **"Make every existing section work harder."**

The page should have one psychological arc:

### **Curiosity**

"Who is this person?"

↓

### **Recognition**

"He's describing exactly what I'm dealing with."

↓

### **Safety**

"He won't pressure me."

↓

### **Trust**

"He has a clear way of working."

↓

### **Confidence**

"I understand what happens if I contact him."

↓

### **Action**

"Let me message him."

That's the journey I would build.

And one final thing I want to emphasize: **I think your "AI GANA" personal brand is strongest when you position yourself as a real estate guide, not merely a property seller.** The phrase you already have, *"Before I recommend a property, I try to understand the person,"* is the seed of that entire brand.

I would build the whole page around that idea.

You don't need 10 services. You don't need 15 sections. You don't need to sound like a massive real estate corporation.

You need the visitor to finish the page thinking:

> **"This is someone I can talk to about property without feeling like I'm walking into a sales trap."**

If you can make the page create *that* feeling, you've got something much more valuable than a conventional real estate website.
